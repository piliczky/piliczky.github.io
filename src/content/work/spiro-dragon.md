This project started as a study of form, anatomy, and stylised surface detail. The Spiro Dragon sculpt was developed entirely in ZBrush, with a focus on expressive shapes, flowing horns, and a strong silhouette.

I used vertex painting rather than UV mapping, so the final result exists only as a high-poly sculpt. That means the visuals are driven by brushwork and surface detail instead of texture UVs, and the model is best appreciated through renders like the ones shown here.

![Alternate view of the Spiro Dragon sculpt](/assets/Spiro_Dragon/Dragon_2.png)

The project was completed over about two weeks during my studies at Flinders University and CDW Studios. It was a fun and rewarding exercise that helped me level up my sculpting workflow, character design decisions, and understanding of form in a digital sculpting pipeline.

I’m looking forward to taking this one further, including the possibility of a 3D print in the future.
